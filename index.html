    About

    I'm 22, in my final year of Electronics & Computer Engineering at Thapar Institute, and most of what I build sits at the seam between AI systems and the infrastructure that has to carry them.

    I like the parts that don't demo well: the retry logic, the schema that stops a pipeline from silently corrupting itself, the index that turns a slow query into a fast one. Agents are only interesting to me once they're reliable enough to hand to someone who didn't build them.

    Outside of shipping, I've spent a lot of hours on competitive programming. 600+ problems, and a Codeforces Master's camp where I landed in the Hall of Fame for matrix exponentiation and sqrt decomposition solutions. That instinct for complexity shows up in everything I write.
